Neocortical circuits underlying visually-guided behaviors in mice

This project was funded by EU Horizon 2020.

Funded by a European Fellowship from the Marie Curie Action of the EU, we sought to understand how different brain regions, specifically within the cerebral cortex, work together to generate productive behavior. Which neurons in which areas of cortex cooperate to give rise to our ability to perceive, to think, and to act? How do they achieve this cooperation?

So far, we have succeeded in measuring the activity of thousands of neurons across the cortex of mice using a novel electrode array recording technology called Neuropixels. Moreover, we have employed state-of-the-art techniques for inactivating different sets of cortical neurons, to study the role of different parts of cortex in behavior. We have performed these experiments in the context of a sophisticated beahvioral task for rodents that allows us to precisely quantify the perceptual and cognitive factors that underlie the behaviors of the mice.

Expanding on original plans, this project came to involve additional approaches for activity across cortical areas, including widefield calcium imaging. As part of the project, we have developed new algorithms for processing electrophysiological and imaging data, and have shared these resources open-source.

We have shared some datasets recorded as part of this project at data.cortexlab.net.
